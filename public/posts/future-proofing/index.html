Future-proofing software

August 14, 2020 One-minute read

(0.1)

Overengineering a bad or wrong design makes future-proofing that much more difficult. Like reinforcing a home to withstand a hurricane, using stronger materials will only allow the structure to withstand winds up to a certain magnitude. To withstand stronger winds the entire design needs to change, necessitating razing the home to its foundations and starting again.

Future-proofing is not about adding a features that you assume your users may ask for in the future. These features will go unused, effectively cluttering your business logic (requiring testing prior to each release, or being hidden by sweeping under a carpet of feature flags), or most likely when the feature is requested the requirements will be so different as to necessitate a rewrite.

Future-proofing is removing all unnecessary features.

  • Minimize dependencies.

  • Don’t use a framework.

  • Use technologies that have a history of maintaining backwards compatibility.

  • Choose a stable platform to develop in.

  • The UNIX philosophy
